Mentor

The primary motivation behind Dr. Asthana’s research is the observation that memory helps individuals to handle everyday challenges. It aids an individual in providing relevant information storage and retrieval at the time of need. Memory provides critical information from remembering appointments made with the doctor to the navigation of our external world. Dr. Asthana’s ongoing research aims to understand the psychological, physiological, and cognitive aspects during memory consolidation and reconsolidation mechanisms. Current research direction combines behavior, physiological responses, cognitive, and virtual reality – include: (Biopac), and DC-stimulator (Solterix). Dr. Asthana has planned and designed several memory studies using these tools.
